How the Model Works

The Malaria Detection App uses a YOLO (You Only Look Once) model, which is a state-of-the-art object detection model, to identify malaria-infected cells in blood smear images. Below is an overview of how the model works:

1. Image Upload

Users upload an image of a blood smear, which is processed by the app. The image must be clear and of good quality to ensure accurate detection.

2. Model Processing

Once the image is uploaded, it is passed through a pre-trained YOLO model. The model analyzes the image in real-time to locate and classify objects, such as trophozoites and white blood cells (WBCs), which are indicators of malaria infection.

3. Detection Results

The YOLO model outputs bounding boxes around detected objects, along with labels and confidence scores. These results are overlaid on the original image and displayed to the user, highlighting the detected cells.

4. Model Training

The model has been trained using a large dataset of annotated blood smear images. It learns to recognize patterns and features associated with malaria-infected cells, allowing it to make accurate predictions on new, unseen images.

5. Limitations

While the model is highly effective, its accuracy depends on the quality of the input image and the variability of the blood smears. Blurry or low-quality images may result in false negatives or incorrect classifications.
